Introduction to Engineering Drawing

Objective:

To study the use of various instruments required in Engineering Drawing


Instruments used:


 Components of Engineering Drawing:

  1. Drawing Board

  2. Drawing Sheet

  3. Title Block

  4. Mini Drafter

  5. Set up Drawing table

  6. Pencils

  7. Set square

  8. Lines

  9. Compass

  • Made of well seasoned softwood of about 25 mm thick.
  • The standard size of board depends on the size of drawing sheet size required. 

  • The standard drawing sheet sizes are arrived at on the basic Principal of x: y = 1: 2^ (1/2) and xy = 1 where x and y are the sides of the sheet.
  • For class work, use of A2 size drawing sheet is preferred.

  • The title block should lie within the drawing space at the bottom right hand comer of the sheet.
  • The title block can have a maximum length of 170 mm and width of 65mm.
 Click on the drafter to place it.
  • Mini-drafter consists of an angle formed by two arms with scales marked and rigidly hinged to each other.
  • It is used for drawing horizontal, vertical and inclined lines, parallel and perpendicular lines and for measuring lines and angles.
 Place drawing sheet  Click on clips and mini drafter to place them
Drawing Clips:
  • To fix the drawing sheet to the board clips are used.
  • Clips are used at all the four corners of the drawing board to clamp the paper.
 Setup Done

  • HB Soft grade for Border lines, lettering and free sketching
  • H Medium grade for Visible outlines, visible edges and boundary lines.
  • 2H Hard grade for construction lines, Dimension lines, Leader lines, Extension lines, Centre lines, Hatching lines and Hidden lines.

  • A set square is an object used in engineering and technical drawing, with the aim of providing a straightedge at a right angle or other particular planar angle to a baseline.
  • The setsquare with 60°-30° of 250 mm long and 45° of 200mm long is convenient for use.
 Types of lines in Engineering Drawing:

  1. Continuous Narrow Lines

  2. Continuous Wide Lines

  3. Continuous Narrow Lines with Zigzags

  4. Dashed Narrow Lines

  5. Long-Dashed Dotted Narrow Lines

  6. Long-Dashed Dotted Wide Lines

  • A compass is a technical drawing instrument that can be used for inscribing circles or arcs.
  • Normally, a large compass is used in engineering drawing.
  • It has a knee joint in one leg that permits the insertion of pen or pencil point or attaching lengthening bar with pen or pencil point attached to it.
